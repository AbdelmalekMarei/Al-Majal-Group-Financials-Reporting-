Insight 1 — Zero Cash Despite Profit: NGK generated $106.5K net income YTD, yet the cash balance is $0. The entire profit (and more) is consumed by working capital expansion — a classic "profitable but cash-poor" scenario. The Cash Conversion Cycle of ~257 days means it takes over 8 months from purchasing inventory to receiving cash from customers.
Insight 2 — Intercompany is the #1 Drain: The $1.52M due from Allied represents 28.8% of total assets — the single largest cash drain. This intercompany receivable alone could fund 3.7 months of operations if settled. Urgent intercompany reconciliation and settlement is the highest-impact action for liquidity.
Insight 3 — Inventory Overstocking: At 253 Days Inventory Outstanding, NGK carries nearly 9 months of inventory. The warehouse stock of $1.74M (with only $93.7K provisioned for slow movers) suggests significant overstocking. A 20% reduction would free ~$347K in cash. Review purchasing patterns and demand forecasting urgently.
Insight 4 — GRNI Offsets Transit Risk: The $1.25M Stock in Transit is fully offset by GRNI on the liabilities side, meaning the net cash impact is neutral. However, once invoices are received, this converts to real AP that will need cash settlement — creating a future cash pressure point.
Insight 5 — GP Margin Improving: Despite the cash crunch, the operational profitability is sound — YTD GP margin of 19.0% is the best in years (vs. 17.5% in 2025). The problem is structural (too much capital locked in working capital), not operational. Fix the balance sheet, and the P&L will generate real cash.
